Automating Tasks in Revit: Dynamo and Add-ins

Published on March 19, 2024 by RevitPro

In any complex project, repetitive tasks can consume a significant amount of time and introduce the potential for human error. Revit's power can be extended exponentially by leveraging automation tools. By automating routine tasks, you can free up valuable time to focus on what truly matters: design. The two primary methods for automation in Revit are Dynamo and third-party add-ins.

Introduction to Dynamo for Revit

Dynamo is an open-source visual programming platform that installs with Revit. It allows users to create custom scripts or "graphs" to automate workflows without needing traditional programming experience. Dynamo uses a simple, node-based interface where you connect blocks (nodes) to build a logical sequence of actions.

What can you do with Dynamo?

  • Manage Data: Automatically read data from spreadsheets, modify element parameters in bulk, and export project data to custom formats.
  • Create Complex Geometry: Generate complex geometric forms, patterns, and facades that would be difficult or impossible to model manually.
  • Automate Modeling Tasks: Place thousands of elements based on rules, renumber rooms or doors, and manage sheets and views automatically.
  • Perform Analysis: Connect with analysis software to perform structural, solar, or other environmental analyses directly from your Revit model.

A simple example of a Dynamo script could be one that reads room names and numbers from an Excel file and automatically creates corresponding rooms with the correct data in your Revit project, saving hours of manual data entry.

Leveraging Revit Add-ins

Beyond Dynamo, a vast ecosystem of third-party add-ins (or plugins) exists to enhance Revit's functionality. These tools are developed by other companies and can be installed directly into Revit, adding new buttons and features to the interface. They often provide user-friendly solutions for specific, complex problems.

Popular Categories of Add-ins:

  • Modeling and Productivity: Tools that speed up modeling tasks, like creating bulk selections, managing families, or aligning elements.
  • Interoperability: Add-ins that improve how Revit communicates with other software platforms (e.g., Rhino, SketchUp, or various analysis tools).
  • Data Management: Advanced tools for managing parameters, schedules, and other data within large projects.
  • Annotation and Documentation: Plugins that automate the creation of tags, dimensions, and sheets, ensuring consistency across construction documents.

While some add-ins are free, many are paid products. However, the time saved by a good add-in can often provide a significant return on investment, especially for large firms or complex projects.

Getting Started with Automation

If you're new to automation in Revit, here's a simple path to get started:

  1. Identify Repetitive Tasks: Look for tasks in your daily workflow that are time-consuming and rule-based. Are you renaming sheets? Placing hundreds of similar components? Managing parameter data? These are perfect candidates for automation.
  2. Explore the Add-in Market: Visit the Autodesk App Store to see what tools are available. You might find a pre-built solution that solves your exact problem.
  3. Learn Dynamo Basics: Start with simple Dynamo scripts. There are countless free tutorials available online (including on the official Dynamo BIM website). Begin by manipulating data before moving on to creating geometry. The "Dynamo Player," included with Revit, allows you to easily run scripts with a simple interface.

By embracing automation, you're not just working faster; you're working smarter. Investing a small amount of time to learn these tools can fundamentally change your workflow, improve accuracy, and allow you to push the boundaries of your designs.
