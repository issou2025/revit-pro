What is Autodesk Revit?

Discover the revolutionary Building Information Modeling software that transformed the AEC industry

The Evolution of Revit

Revit was first developed by Charles River Software in 1997, founded by Leonid Raiz and Irwin Jungreis. The name "Revit" comes from "Revise-Instantly," reflecting its parametric change engine that allows automatic coordination of changes made anywhere in the model.

In 2002, Autodesk acquired Revit Technology Corporation, recognizing the potential of this revolutionary approach to building design and documentation. Since then, Revit has become the industry standard for Building Information Modeling (BIM).

Core Technology

Parametric Engine

Revit's parametric change engine ensures that all elements in your model are interconnected and automatically update when changes are made.

Family Editor

Create custom building components with intelligent behavior using Revit's powerful family editor, allowing for unlimited design possibilities.

Multi-user Collaboration

Enable multiple team members to work on the same project simultaneously with worksets and cloud collaboration features.

Transforming Workflow

Revit has revolutionized how buildings are designed, constructed, and managed. By enabling Building Information Modeling (BIM), Revit has:

  • Improved collaboration between disciplines
  • Reduced errors and coordination issues
  • Enabled better cost estimation and planning
  • Facilitated sustainable design practices
  • Streamlined construction documentation
