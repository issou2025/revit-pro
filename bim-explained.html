Understanding BIM

Building Information Modeling (BIM) is a collaborative process that allows architecture, engineering, and construction professionals to efficiently plan, design, and construct buildings and infrastructure.

BIM Fundamentals

What is BIM?

BIM is not just 3D modeling; it's a process that provides architecture, engineering, and construction professionals with the insight and tools to more efficiently plan, design, construct, and manage buildings and infrastructure.

Key Benefits

  • Improved project visualization
  • Better coordination and clash detection
  • Accurate cost estimation
  • Enhanced project collaboration
  • Reduced errors and rework

BIM Dimensions

  • 3D - Spatial visualization
  • 4D - Time integration
  • 5D - Cost estimation
  • 6D - Sustainability
  • 7D - Facility management

BIM Standards & Guidelines

ISO 19650

International standard for managing information over the whole life cycle of a built asset using building information modeling.

Level of Development (LOD)

Specifications that enable practitioners to specify and articulate with a high degree of clarity the content and reliability of BIM models.

National BIM Standards

Country-specific guidelines and requirements for BIM implementation in construction projects.

File Formats

Industry-standard file formats including IFC, BCF, and COBie for interoperability between different BIM platforms.
