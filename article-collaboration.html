Collaboration in Revit: Worksharing and Cloud Solutions

Published on March 19, 2024 by RevitPro

Modern architectural projects are rarely a solo effort. They require seamless collaboration between architects, engineers, designers, and other stakeholders. Revit is designed from the ground up to support this teamwork through powerful worksharing and cloud-based solutions, ensuring that everyone is working from the most current and coordinated information.

Understanding Revit Worksharing

Worksharing is a fundamental feature in Revit that allows multiple team members to work on the same project model simultaneously. It works by creating a central model, which is the master copy of the project file stored on a network server. Each user then creates a local copy of this central model on their own computer.

Users work on their local copies and can periodically "Synchronize with Central" to publish their changes back to the central model and receive updates from other team members. This process prevents conflicts and ensures that the project remains coordinated.

Key Concepts of Worksharing:

  • Central Model: The master project file that stores all current data and ownership information.
  • Local Models: Personal copies of the central model where users do their daily work.
  • Worksets: Collections of elements in a project (like "Core & Shell," "Interior Partitions," or "MEP Systems"). Users "check out" a workset to gain exclusive editing rights, preventing others from modifying those elements at the same time.
  • Synchronize with Central (Sync): The process of sending your changes to the central model and receiving the latest updates from others.

The Evolution to Cloud Collaboration

While traditional worksharing is effective for teams in a single office, the rise of remote work and global project teams has made cloud-based collaboration essential. Autodesk Construction Cloud (formerly BIM 360) extends Revit's capabilities by hosting the central model in the cloud.

Autodesk Construction Cloud (ACC) & BIM Collaborate Pro

With tools like BIM Collaborate Pro, the central model lives on the cloud, not on a local office server. This means anyone with an internet connection and the proper permissions can access and work on the project from anywhere in the world.

  • Anytime, Anywhere Access: Team members can work on the Revit model from any location, breaking down geographical barriers.
  • No IT Headaches: It eliminates the need for complex and expensive VPNs or dedicated office servers to manage the central model.
  • Live Linking: Consultants using other Autodesk tools (like Civil 3D or Plant 3D) can link their models directly into the Revit project, ensuring everyone is working with the latest designs.
  • Advanced Tools: ACC provides a suite of tools beyond just model hosting, including issue tracking, design review, clash detection, and document management, creating a common data environment (CDE) for the entire project.

Best Practices for Revit Collaboration

To ensure a smooth collaborative workflow, follow these best practices:

  1. Plan Your Worksets: Before starting, agree on a logical workset structure. This prevents confusion and ownership conflicts.
  2. Sync Frequently: Synchronize with the central model often (at least every hour or two) to keep your local copy up-to-date and to release your changes to the team. Always add a comment to your syncs describing your changes.
  3. Communicate with Your Team: Use built-in tools or external communication platforms to let your team know what you are working on, especially if you need to borrow an element from someone else's workset.
  4. Keep Models Lean: A large, bloated model will be slow to sync. Regularly audit and purge your models, and use links for external files instead of importing them directly.

By leveraging Revit's native worksharing and powerful cloud solutions, project teams can achieve a level of collaboration that was previously impossible, leading to more efficient workflows, fewer errors, and ultimately, better buildings.
