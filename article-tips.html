Top 10 Revit Tips for Beginners

Published on March 19, 2024 by RevitPro

Starting your journey with Autodesk Revit can be both exciting and overwhelming. It's a powerful tool with a steep learning curve. To help you get started on the right foot, we've compiled our top 10 essential tips for Revit beginners. Mastering these fundamentals will help you build confidence and efficiency.

1. Customize Your Keyboard Shortcuts

One of the fastest ways to boost your speed in Revit is to use keyboard shortcuts. While Revit comes with default shortcuts, you can customize them to your liking. Type "KS" to open the Keyboard Shortcuts dialog. Set up two-letter shortcuts for your most frequently used commands (e.g., "VV" for Visibility/Graphics, "DL" for Detail Line). This small change will save you countless clicks.

2. Understand the Project Browser

The Project Browser is your command center. Take the time to understand its structure. It organizes all your views, schedules, sheets, families, and groups. Learn to right-click on views to duplicate them, and create custom browser organization schemes to group your views logically (e.g., by discipline or by floor level).

3. Use the "Thin Lines" Tool (TL)

When you're zoomed in on a detailed area, line weights can make it difficult to see what you're doing. Use the keyboard shortcut "TL" to toggle Thin Lines on and off. This displays all lines at a single, thin weight, making it much easier to select and modify elements precisely.

4. Don't Ignore Warnings

Revit's warning messages are there for a reason. Don't just click them away. Click on the warning to expand it and use the "Show" button to locate the problematic elements in your model. Resolving warnings as they appear will prevent major issues and model corruption down the line.

5. Pin Important Elements

Once you've placed critical elements like grids, levels, or linked CAD/Revit files, use the "Pin" tool (shortcut: PN) to lock them in place. This prevents them from being accidentally moved or deleted, which can cause major problems in your model.

6. Master the Visibility/Graphics Overrides (VV/VG)

The Visibility/Graphics Overrides dialog (shortcut: VV or VG) is one of the most powerful tools in Revit. It gives you complete control over how elements appear in each view. You can turn categories on or off, and override their projection lines, cut lines, patterns, and transparency without affecting any other view.

7. Use Temporary Dimensions

When you select an element, Revit displays temporary dimensions to nearby objects. Don't just look at themâ€”use them! You can click on the blue dimension value to type in a precise distance. You can also drag the small blue grips to change what the dimension is referencing.

8. Create and Use View Templates

A View Template is a collection of saved view properties (like scale, detail level, and visibility settings). Once you've set up a view exactly how you like it, you can create a template from it and apply it to other views. This is the key to ensuring consistency across all your drawings and saving a huge amount of time.

9. Learn to Use the Section Box

Working in a complex 3D view can be difficult. In a 3D view, go to the Properties palette and check the "Section Box" parameter. This creates a box around your model that you can push, pull, and resize to isolate specific areas. It's an invaluable tool for understanding and working on complex parts of your building.

10. Purge Your Model Regularly

As you work, your Revit project can accumulate a lot of unused families, materials, and other data, which can increase file size. Periodically, go to the Manage tab and use the "Purge Unused" tool. This will clean up your project and help maintain good performance. Be sure to check what you are purging before you commit!
